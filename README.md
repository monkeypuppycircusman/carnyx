Carnyx — and the boar is screaming.
The background is a battlefield at dusk — dark grey-brown sky, a distant fire glow on the horizon, and embers and ash drifting upward through the air. Waveform gradient sweeps bronze gold into blood red. Cursor is a spear tip.

The instrument is a full SVG hand-drawn carnyx in horizontal layout — the long S-curve bronze tube running from mouthpiece on the right all the way up and left to the boar head. Bronze ring reinforcements at every joint along the tube. A hammered seam line. The tube catches its own highlight at the top.
The boar head is the whole thing — massive skull, a ridgeback bristle crest with individual hair lines, two curved tusks (upper and lower, both with highlight glints), wide flat snout with two nostrils, and the jaw is a separate SVG group that animates open when you play. Inside the open mouth: three upper teeth, three lower teeth, and a red tongue that extends out on blow. The eye is a layered circle with a blood-red iris, a dark pupil, and a golden catchlight — war paint slash marks beneath and above it. Celtic knotwork engraving on the skull. Blood-mark war paint on the face. When you play, a war glow pulses red around the open mouth and three blast rings expand outward from the bell.

The acoustics are accurate — a long bronze tube is a closed cylindrical resonator (odd harmonics) with a brass lip buzz as the source. The Brass knob adds the sawtooth waveshaper for that metallic buzz character. The Growl knob is historically documented: the Tintignac carnyxes had a bronze rattle inside the boar head that vibrated sympathetically with the note — confirmed by archaeologists. It adds a low subharmonic flutter that makes it rougher and wilder. The Roar knob adds an explosive noise burst at the attack.
